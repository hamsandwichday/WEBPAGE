Public Recreation Athletics National Training (PRANT)

Mission and Goals

The Public Recreation Athletics National Training (PRANT) interest group aims to enhance and promote students to recreational athletics through comprehensive national training programs. The primary goals include:


Quality Training Programs: Develop and implement standardized, high-quality training programs for individuals involved in public recreation athletics, including coaches, officials, and volunteers.
Accessibility: Ensure that training programs are accessible to individuals of all backgrounds, regardless of socioeconomic status, geography, or other barriers, to promote inclusivity and equal opportunities in sports.
Safety Standards: Advocate for and establish national safety standards in recreational athletics, minimizing the risk of injuries and promoting a secure environment for participants.
Infrastructure Improvement: Lobby for investments in public recreation facilities, focusing on infrastructure improvement, maintenance, and the creation of spaces that cater to a wide range of athletic activities.
Community Engagement: Foster community engagement through outreach programs, encouraging people of all ages to participate in recreational athletics and promoting the physical and mental health benefits associated with an active lifestyle.

Importance of PRANT

Health and Well-being: PRANT contributes to the overall health and well-being of the population by promoting physical activity and a healthy lifestyle.
Community Building: Recreational athletics provide opportunities for community building, fostering social connections and a sense of belonging.
Skill Development: Training programs enhance the skills of coaches, officials, and participants, raising the overall standard of recreational athletics in the nation.
Safety: Establishing safety standards ensures the well-being of participants, minimizing the risk of injuries and creating a safe environment for all.

Lobbying Efforts

PRANT will lobby at both federal and state levels to garner support for its initiatives. Target groups include:
Legislators: Engage with lawmakers to advocate for funding and policies that support public recreation and athletics training programs.
Government Agencies: Collaborate with relevant government agencies responsible for sports and recreation to ensure alignment with national goals.
Educational Institutions: Partner with schools, colleges, and universities to integrate training programs into educational curricula and extracurricular activities.
Local Communities: Work with local governments and community leaders to promote the importance of public recreation athletics and secure support for infrastructure improvements.

Interest Group Category:

PRANT falls under the category of "Recreation and Sports Advocacy."

Membership Goals

PRANT aims to gather a diverse membership base comprising individuals involved in recreational athletics, including coaches, athletes, officials, and anyone passionate about promoting public access to sports. The goal is to have at least 10,000 members within the first year, representing a broad spectrum of stakeholders committed to advancing the mission of PRANT.
